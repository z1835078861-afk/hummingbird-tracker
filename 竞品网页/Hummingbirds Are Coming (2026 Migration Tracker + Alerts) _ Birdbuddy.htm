Don't just track them. See them.

Here's how to make sure they stick around when they reach you.

STEP 1

Set up early

Put your feeder out 1–2 weeks before hummingbirds are expected in your area. They’re scouting for reliable food sources, be the first one they find.

STEP 2

Make fresh nectar

Mix 1 part white sugar with 4 parts water. No red dye, ever. Change it every 3–5 days, more often in hot weather. Clean feeders thoroughly each time you refill.

STEP 3

Pick the right spot

Hang your feeder in a shaded area near flowers or shrubs. Hummingbirds like to perch nearby between feedings, and shade keeps nectar fresh longer.

Wonder-worthy

Fun facts that'll make you love these tiny travellers even more.

Their wings flap 15–80 times per second — and over 200 during courtship dives.

A hummingbird’s heart beats up to 1,260 times per minute during flight.

Many hummingbirds gain 25–40% of their body weight before starting migration.

They eat every 10–15 minutes, visiting up to 2,000 flowers a day.

Hummingbirds are the only birds that can fly backwards.

Ruby-throated hummingbirds cross the Gulf of Mexico in a single 18–20 hour flight.
